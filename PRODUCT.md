# Forge Product Foundation

## What Is Forge?

Forge is an open, modular digital operations platform for Exercise Control.

Forge helps exercise teams plan, execute, review, and archive military training exercises by connecting operational events, injects, products, controllers, reviews, observations, relationships, and audit records inside one exercise-centered system.

## Who Is Forge For?

Forge is for:

- Exercise Directors.
- Exercise Control teams.
- Intelligence Chiefs.
- Controllers.
- Reviewers.
- Observer teams.
- Training command leaders.
- Exercise planners.
- System administrators.
- Developers building exercise-control capability.

## What Problems Does Forge Solve?

Forge reduces administrative friction so Exercise Control teams can spend more time planning, executing, and improving military training rather than managing disconnected spreadsheets, documents, and workflows.

Forge helps solve:

- Disconnected exercise data.
- Manual inject and product tracking.
- Weak review traceability.
- Unclear relationship between objectives, injects, products, observations, and AAR findings.
- Fragmented timelines.
- Product review bottlenecks.
- Difficult archive and after-action reconstruction.
- Repeated manual setup across exercises.

## What Forge Intentionally Does Not Solve

Forge is not:

- A replacement for human operational judgment.
- A live intelligence system.
- A production authentication or identity provider in the current foundation stage.
- A system for bypassing review authority.
- A generic office automation suite.
- A tool for publishing uncontrolled exercise products.
- A replacement for commanders, controllers, observers, or training audiences.
- A system that should blur real-world information and exercise fiction.

## Mission Statement

Every Event.

Every Exercise.

## Product Philosophy

Forge is built around a simple belief:

People create training. Forge manages information.

The product should make the right operational action easier, faster, clearer, and more auditable without removing human authority.

## Core Design Principles

| Principle | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| Humans remain in command. | Forge assists, but people decide. |
| Machine assistance never replaces operational judgment. | Automation supports planning, drafting, validation, and traceability. |
| Every operational decision is auditable. | Important actions should leave clear records. |
| Everything belongs to an Exercise. | Exercise context anchors workflows, data, permissions, and archive. |
| Operational Assets are first-class citizens. | Objectives, injects, products, controllers, observations, and findings are core product objects. |
| Relationships are as important as data. | Forge must explain how assets connect, not merely list them. |
| Documentation evolves with the software. | Features are not complete when users cannot learn them. |
| Design for operators under pressure. | Interfaces should support repeated operational use, scanning, and quick decisions. |
| Reduce clicks. | Frequent tasks should require less navigation and fewer manual steps. |
| Reduce administrative burden. | Forge should replace duplicated tracking and hand-maintained coordination artifacts. |
| Open architecture. | Services, plugins, profiles, and future integrations should remain understandable and replaceable. |
| Modularity. | Capabilities should have clear boundaries and testable behavior. |
| Accessibility. | Forge should support keyboard access, readable contrast, and clear structure. |
| Operational realism. | Training examples, workflows, and reference data should feel like real exercise work while remaining fictional and safe. |

## Long-Term Platform Vision

Forge should become the operating system for modern Exercise Control:

- Plan exercises in Atlas.
- Execute from Mission Control.
- Manage injects, timeline events, products, review queues, and controller assignments.
- Connect all operational assets in the Knowledge Graph.
- Preserve audit trails and review records.
- Support after-action review and archive.
- Enable reusable profiles, plugins, workflows, and product templates.
- Support future Mission Replay, operational analytics, and bounded AI assistance.

## Definition Of Success

Forge succeeds when:

- Controllers spend less time reconciling disconnected documents.
- Exercise Directors can see readiness and risk quickly.
- Injects and products remain tied to objectives and review decisions.
- AAR teams can trace findings to evidence.
- Developers have clear product standards and reference data.
- Forge Academy teaches realistic workflows using Project Sentinel.
- Every major feature can be demonstrated against Project Sentinel.
