# Project Forge Vision

## Why Project Forge Exists

Project Forge exists to help Exercise Control (EXCON) teams convert fast-moving real-world signals into credible, scenario-consistent exercise material without losing control of the training narrative.

Modern exercises often require controllers to respond to current events, official statements, social media narratives, and operational reporting at a tempo that exceeds manual drafting capacity. At the same time, every product released into an exercise must respect the scenario, preserve fiction boundaries, support training objectives, and remain reviewable by human controllers.

Forge is intended to become a disciplined platform for that work: a system that helps EXCON teams reason from real-world material to notional exercise products while preserving traceability, consistency, and human release authority.

## Problem Statement

EXCON teams face a recurring operational gap:

- Real-world information moves quickly.
- Exercise scenarios require controlled, fictionalized representations of that information.
- Human controllers must preserve scenario continuity while producing useful products under time pressure.
- Existing workflows often rely on disconnected notes, manual copy-editing, local templates, and individual institutional knowledge.
- AI-assisted drafting can accelerate production, but unmanaged AI use can introduce scenario drift, unsupported details, policy issues, and weak auditability.

Project Forge addresses this gap by separating source intake, scenario context, deterministic translation, reasoning support, product preparation, QA, and human review into clear platform services.

## Vision

Project Forge will be the EXCON platform for transforming real-world signals into controlled, notional, scenario-aligned exercise products.

The platform should allow a controller to ask:

> What would this real-world signal look like inside our exercise world?

Forge should then help assemble the relevant scenario context, translate real-world terms into exercise terms, prepare product-specific material, check quality and fiction boundaries, and route the result to human review before release.

## Goals

- Preserve scenario fidelity across all generated or prepared products.
- Keep EXCON staff in control of approval, release, correction, and escalation.
- Maintain source traceability from real-world signal to notional exercise product.
- Support reusable exercise profiles for different training venues, units, and scenarios.
- Support product plugins for different report types and presentation formats.
- Provide deterministic local foundations for validation, translation, orchestration, and QA.
- Make future AI provider integrations replaceable, testable, and bounded by policy.
- Enable auditable workflows that leadership, controllers, and developers can understand.

## Non-Goals

- Forge is not a system for publishing real-world intelligence.
- Forge is not intended to replace controller judgment or release authority.
- Forge is not a live collection platform in its current foundation stage.
- Forge is not a general-purpose chatbot.
- Forge is not a report generator that bypasses human review.
- Forge is not designed to erase the distinction between real-world events and exercise fiction.
- Forge should not require a specific AI provider, hosting model, or document export stack.

## Benefits To Exercise Control

Project Forge should provide direct benefits to EXCON teams:

- **Speed**: Reduces the manual effort required to prepare scenario-aligned drafts.
- **Consistency**: Applies common profiles, dictionaries, entity mappings, control measures, and QA checks.
- **Traceability**: Preserves the source, assumptions, scenario references, and transformation path behind each product.
- **Control**: Keeps human controllers as the approval authority and final editorial gate.
- **Reuse**: Allows profiles, plugins, workflows, and QA rules to be reused across exercise events.
- **Scalability**: Supports multiple product types and workflows without forcing every team to rebuild the same process.
- **Risk reduction**: Helps identify missing metadata, real-world leakage, low confidence, and scenario inconsistency before products enter play.

## Leadership Summary

Project Forge is a platform investment in disciplined exercise production. It is not only an AI tool; it is a control framework for using automation responsibly inside EXCON workflows.

The desired end state is faster product development, stronger scenario control, clearer review authority, and more reliable support to training audiences.
