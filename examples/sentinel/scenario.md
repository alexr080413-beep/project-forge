# Sentinel Scenario

## Overview

Mountain Exercise 3-27 places an infantry battalion into a fictional high-altitude security operation against a near-peer threat proxy operating in rugged terrain. The exercise stresses intelligence fusion, controller coordination, command decisions, logistics, medical support, information environment activity, weather adaptation, public affairs, and observer assessment.

## Operational Setting

The training audience is operating in a fictional mountain border region with steep terrain, narrow roads, limited communications, severe weather, and a civilian population under stress from displacement, misinformation, and disrupted services.

## Threat

The opposing force is a near-peer aligned irregular element with:

- Reconnaissance teams.
- Small unmanned aircraft.
- Electronic warfare capability.
- Information operations support.
- Local proxy influence networks.
- Cyber disruption capability.

## Civilian Population

The civilian population includes:

- Mountain village leaders.
- Displaced families.
- Local medical personnel.
- Contractors moving supplies.
- Civil authorities requesting support.
- Media stringers and simulated social media actors.

## Information Environment

The scenario includes:

- Rumors about civilian harm.
- Misleading route safety claims.
- Simulated social media pressure.
- Public affairs questions.
- Local leader concerns.

## Weather Impacts

Weather affects:

- Visibility.
- Route movement.
- Casualty evacuation.
- ISR collection.
- Resupply timing.
- Cold-weather injuries.

## ISR Collection

ISR collection includes:

- Small unmanned aircraft reports.
- Observation post reporting.
- Signals disruption indicators.
- Route reconnaissance.
- Civilian pattern-of-life reporting.

## Cyber Activity

Cyber injects include:

- GPS degradation.
- Logistics tracker outage.
- Spoofed message traffic.
- Public website defacement.
- Controller-approved simulated network degradation.

## Logistics And Medical Support

Logistics and medical support challenges include:

- Fuel convoy delay.
- Snow-chain shortage.
- Route closure.
- Medical evacuation delay.
- Warming tent generator failure.

## Public Affairs

Public affairs activity includes:

- Media interview request.
- Draft commander statement.
- Correction of false claims.
- Civilian assistance messaging.

## Observer Teams

Observer teams track:

- Decision timing.
- Controller coordination.
- Objective coverage.
- Product quality.
- Review discipline.
- Evidence for AAR findings.
