# Brand Vision

Forge exists to give Exercise Control teams a modern digital operating layer for complex training events.

## Positioning

Primary positioning:

> Open-Source Digital Operations Platform for Exercise Control

Aspirational positioning:

> The Operating System for Modern Exercise Control

The primary positioning should be used in GitHub, documentation, technical briefs, website copy, and public project descriptions. The aspirational positioning should be used selectively in executive one-pagers, pitch decks, and long-term vision slides.

## Mission

Forge modernizes Exercise Control by providing an open, modular platform that connects planning, execution, intelligence, assessment, and after-action review into one operational workflow.

## Vision

Become the digital operating system for military exercise control.

## Core Philosophy

People create training. Forge manages information.

Forge should be presented as a disciplined control framework, not a replacement for controller judgment. The platform helps organize source material, scenario truth, product workflows, review queues, audit trails, and assessment records so human teams can operate faster without losing authority.

## Strategic Narrative

Military exercises are information-intensive. Controllers must manage real-world signals, exercise truth, training objectives, injects, products, reviews, approvals, and after-action records under time pressure.

Forge provides a common digital foundation for that work. It connects the exercise lifecycle, controller workspaces, scenario context, product generation, human review, metrics, audit records, and archives into one coherent operating model.

## Brand Promise

Forge helps Exercise Control teams preserve control, increase tempo, and maintain traceability across the full exercise lifecycle.

## What Forge Is

- A modular Exercise Control platform.
- A common operational workflow for planning, execution, intelligence, review, assessment, and archive.
- A deterministic service foundation for scenario-safe product preparation.
- A future Forge Studio command-center experience.
- An open architecture for profiles, plugins, workflows, and controlled integrations.

## What Forge Is Not

- Forge is not a chatbot.
- Forge is not a system for publishing real-world intelligence.
- Forge is not a replacement for Exercise Control authority.
- Forge is not a generic business dashboard.
- Forge is not an uncontrolled automation layer.
- Forge is not a tool that releases products into play without human review.

## Brand Principles

| Principle | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| Human judgment is authoritative | Controllers approve, reject, correct, and release. Forge supports the workflow. |
| Forge assists; people decide | Machine assistance helps prepare, validate, and organize information, but people retain command. |
| Open architecture | Forge should remain modular, inspectable, extensible, and testable. |
| Mission before technology | Product language should lead with operational outcomes, not tools or novelty. |
| Modular by design | Profiles, plugins, services, and workflows should be explainable as replaceable components. |
| Secure by default | Messaging should emphasize controlled workflows, explicit boundaries, auditability, and safe defaults. |
| Built for military, joint, and coalition exercises | The brand should feel credible for service, joint, and multinational training environments. |
