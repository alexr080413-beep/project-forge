# Presentations

Forge presentations should be clear, operational, and restrained. They should help leaders and technical audiences understand the platform without turning Forge into generic software marketing.

## Presentation Types

| Type | Audience | Goal |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Executive one-pager | Senior defense leaders, training command leaders | Explain mission value, authority, lifecycle integration, and modernization path. |
| Technical one-pager | Developers, architects, platform owners | Explain service boundaries, deterministic foundations, profiles, plugins, and workflows. |
| Pitch deck | Sponsors, stakeholders, partners | Explain problem, solution, architecture, operating model, and roadmap. |
| Product brief | Exercise directors and controller leads | Explain controller workflows, review queues, timelines, and audit trails. |

## Title Slide

Recommended title:

```text
Forge
```

Subtitle:

```text
Open-Source Digital Operations Platform for Exercise Control
```

Optional aspirational line:

```text
The Operating System for Modern Exercise Control
```

Slogan:

```text
Every Event. Every Inject. Every Exercise.
```

Tagline:

```text
Human Command. Machine Assistance.
```

## Visual Style

- Use Black or Graphite backgrounds.
- Use Forge Orange as a controlled accent.
- Use the metallic Forge logo on title and closing slides.
- Use operational diagrams, timelines, and workflow views over generic stock imagery.
- Keep slides clean, dense enough for credibility, but not cluttered.

## Recommended Pitch Deck Flow

1. Forge title and positioning.
2. Exercise Control problem.
3. Current workflow friction.
4. Forge operating model.
5. Exercise lifecycle.
6. Controller workspaces.
7. Signal-to-product workflow.
8. Human review and audit trail.
9. Open architecture: profiles, plugins, services, workflows.
10. Current foundation status.
11. Roadmap.
12. Call to action.

## Slide Copy Examples

Problem:

```text
Exercise Control teams manage fast-moving information, scenario truth, injects, reviews, approvals, and after-action records across disconnected tools.
```

Solution:

```text
Forge connects the exercise lifecycle into one open, modular operational workflow.
```

Human authority:

```text
Forge assists with information management, product preparation, QA, and routing. Controllers decide what enters exercise play.
```

Architecture:

```text
Forge is modular by design: services, profiles, plugins, workflows, audit, metrics, and security are explicit platform boundaries.
```

## Do Not

- Do not lead with AI before the mission problem.
- Do not use generic business stock photos.
- Do not imply Forge replaces human command authority.
- Do not show combat spectacle, explosions, or weapons-focused imagery.
- Do not overload slides with every service name unless the audience is technical.
